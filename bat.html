Bats are the only mammals capable of true flight. They belong to the order Chiroptera and are found all over the world except in extreme deserts and polar regions. There are over 1,400 species of bats, making them the second largest order of mammals.

Bats have specialized wings that allow them to perform a wide range of flight patterns. Their wings are formed from a thin membrane of skin stretched between elongated finger bones, making them highly maneuverable in the air. Unlike birds, which flap their wings up and down, bats use a combination of flapping and gliding, allowing them to fly more efficiently and navigate tight spaces.

Bats use echolocation to navigate and hunt, especially at night. They emit high-frequency sound waves that bounce off objects and return to the batâ€™s ears. By analyzing these echoes, bats can determine the size, shape, and distance of objects in their environment. This ability helps them locate prey, avoid obstacles, and navigate even in complete darkness .

Bats can live up to 30 years in the wild.Some species of bats can eat up to half their body weight in insects every night. Not all bats are nocturnal. Some, like the fruit bat, are active during the day. Bat populations are important for controlling insect populations, especially mosquitoes.
