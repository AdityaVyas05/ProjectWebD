Pigeons are birds that belong to the family Columbidae, which includes around 310 species of birds. They are commonly found in urban and rural areas across the world. Pigeons are known for their ability to navigate long distances and their impressive homing skills, often being used in ancient times for communication.

Pigeons have excellent eyesight and can see ultraviolet light. They can fly at speeds up to 55 miles per hour. Unlike most birds, pigeons can recognize themselves in a mirror. Pigeons have been used in military operations, particularly in World War I and II, for carrying messages.
