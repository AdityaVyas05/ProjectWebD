Crows are highly intelligent birds that belong to the genus Corvus, a part of the family Corvidae. They are found in many parts of the world and are known for their problem-solving abilities, complex social structures, and strong communication skills.

Crows can use tools to access food, something few animals can do. They can recognize human faces and even remember them for years. Crows have an extensive vocabulary of calls and are capable of mimicking sounds from their environment. They are social birds and often form large groups called "murders".

Crows are found in a wide range of habitats, including forests, grasslands, urban areas, and coastal regions. They are adaptable and can thrive in environments modified by humans, such as cities and farmland.

Crows are omnivores, meaning they eat both plant and animal material. They are opportunistic feeders and will eat a variety of foods including seeds, fruits, small animals, and even garbage.
